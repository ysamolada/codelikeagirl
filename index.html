eCommerce-and-buisness

eCommerce websites are websites where people use to directly buy products from. You have probably used many eCommerce websites before, most big brands and plenty of smaller ones have one. Any website that includes a shopping cart and a way for you to provide credit card information to make a purchase falls into this category.

Here are some ideas

  • Beauty and health products
  • Selling Second-hand Products
  • Fashion/Jewellery Products
  • Tech Products
  • Furniture/Decoration Products

Tips for making your eCommerce website even better:

  • Keep it simple
  • Make branding a priority
  • Think like a website visitor
  • Use colour to your advantage
  • Use high-quality images
  • Make your content scannable
  • Make it look professional
  • Make categories easy to navigate

Business websites are websites that are devoted to representing a specific business. E-commerce websites are business websites however, business websites do not sell anything directly. For example, they encourage visitors to get in contact for more information.

entertainment-and-media

Entertainment websites are websites that people visit purely for entertainment purposes. They could be humours websites with fun and interesting content like Buzzfeed. Most of these websites, usually make money through advertisements that show up on their pages. If you want to start an entertainment website there are plenty of options, you can make funny or informative videos, write entertaining blog posts, draw comics, or create fun quizzes.

Media websites are websites that collect news stories or other reporting. There is some overlap with the entertainment websites. However, media websites are more likely to include reported pieces in addition to or instead of content meant purely for entertainment. This category includes sites like the Washington Post website, Slate, and Inc.

    Educational website are websites that have the primary goal of either providing educational materials to visitors or providing information on an educational institution to them. Educational websites can include websites that have games, videos or topic related resources that act as tools to enhance learning and supplement classroom teaching. These websites help make the process of learning entertaining and attractive to the student, especially in today's age.

    Ideas just for you!

    • Anything related to the concept of education
    • Sites that help with school-subjects
    • A wed page with information about historical events and faces
    • Create an online course

    Aim for

    • Accuracy of Information
    • User-Friendly Navigation
    • Simple Design
    • A variety of Images
    • A low Loading Time for your Pages
    • Do not Over Explain Informational Text
    • A Colourful and Exciting website

    personal

    Personal websites are websites that people use in order to put their own thoughts out into the world. This category includes personal blogs, vlogs, and photo diaries people share with the world. These websites are not about money however, they can easily evolve into something that makes some if they become popular enough. Primarily though these sights exist as a way to share your feelings, insights, and art with any friends and strangers that might be interested.

    Here are some ideas

    • Lifestyle related topics
    • Personal blogs
    • Vlogs
    • Photo Diaries people share with the world

    Tips:

    • Make sure your site tells a strong story and reveals your personality.
    • Welcoming Homepage.
    • A Compelling First Page.
    • A Strong Call to Action for Visitors.
    • Consistent Branding

    non-profit

    Non-profit websites are websites that do not aim to sell products or services to their visitors, but they still need to convince people to support a good cause. This kind of websites are one of the primary ways the charity organisations connect with their potential donors.

    Ideas

    • a websites that collects donations and informs you about climate change and all the actions made to stop it
    • a website that supports a movement and collects donations e.g. a website that collects money to support the BLM movement

    Our advice

    • Feature the Most Relevant Information
    • Put the Navigation Bar at the Top
    • Keep Titles Short and Simple
    • Make Drop-Down Menus Easy to Read
    • Provide Multiple Ways to Get to Key Content

    portfolio

    Portfolio websites are websites commonly used from creative professionals and freelancers that are hired based on demonstrated skills. Service providers who want to show potential clients the quality of the work, primarily use a portfolio website to collect some of the best samples of past work they’ve done as they are more focused on a particular task: collecting work samples. In other words, portfolios are websites that are devoted to show examples of past work.

    More Ideas

    • Minimalistic Portfolio
    • Contemporary Portfolio
    • Vintage Portfolio
    • Bohemian Portfolio
