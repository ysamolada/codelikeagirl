    @eCommerce-and-buisness

    An eCommerce website is a website where people use to directly buy products from. You have probably used many eCommerce websites before, most big brands and plenty of smaller ones have one. Any website that includes a shopping cart and a way for you to provide credit card information to make a purchase falls into this category. A business website is any website that is devoted to representing a specific business. E-commerce websites are business websites however, business websites do not sell anything directly. For example, they encourage visitors to get in contact for more information.

    @entertainment-and-media

    An entertainment website is a website that people visit purely for entertainment purposes. They could be humours websites with fun and interesting content like Buzzfeed. Most of these websites, usually make money through advertisements that show up on their pages. If you want to start an entertainment website there are plenty of options, you can make funny or informative videos, write entertaining blog posts, draw comics, or create fun quizzes. Tips: There are many entertainment websites out there, you should anticipate taking some time and work hard to find ideas that will connect you with the audience. A media website is a website that collects news stories or other reporting. There is some overlap with the entertainment websites. However, media websites are more likely to include reported pieces in addition to or instead of content meant purely for entertainment. This category includes sites like the Washington Post website, Slate, and Inc.
